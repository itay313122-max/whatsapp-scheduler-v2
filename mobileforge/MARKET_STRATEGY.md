## The core wedge: working apps, not mockups

Stitch is **design-first** — it produces beautiful UI *pictures*. We are
**function-first** — we produce *working apps*. On the same coffee-shop prompt,
our Coffee Corner shipped with a **real cart** (add/remove, live total), 13
working onClick handlers, and 5 state hooks — quality score **100/100**, every
screen reachable. That's the difference between "a design to hand to a developer"
and "an app you can use right now."

**Positioning:** *"From idea to a working app — not just a picture."*

1. **Real photographs** — code complete; needs `PEXELS_API_KEY` in production.
2. **Raw model polish** — wire Groq (fast) + NVIDIA Qwen-Coder (code-tuned) in

## The offensive plan — capturing the future market

**1. Own "functional generation."** Market the working-app angle relentlessly.
   Every demo shows a cart adding items, a form validating, a screen navigating —
   things a mockup can't do. This is our defensible moat.

**2. Make verified quality a visible promise.** The Quality score + flow map are
   unique. Brand it: *"Every MobileForge app is verified — reachable screens,
   wired controls, no dead UI."* Trust converts.

**3. No lock-in, total portability.** Runnable React project + DESIGN.md +
   live link. Stitch ties you to its ecosystem; we set you free. Developers
   choose the open tool.

**4. Win the markets Stitch ignores.** Native RTL/Hebrew/Arabic and multi-
   language from day one — a wedge into regions Google deprioritizes.

**5. Speed as a feature.** Groq generation in ~10s vs. Stitch's longer cycles.
   Fast iteration loops keep users in flow.

**6. Build the ecosystem.** Template gallery (12 and growing), AI suggestions,
   annotate, version compare — a *workspace*, not a one-shot generator. Depth
   of workflow retains users.

**7. Reliability others can't match.** 6-provider fallback = generation never
   hard-fails. Enterprise-grade dependability as a selling point.
